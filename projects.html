From BI to UX

At the beginning of 2020, I started looking into what direction I wanted my career to go. I have a lot of interest in life and am always aiming to learn, which is awesome, but also leads to me wanting to do too much at once. During this time of exploration, a job posting for a User Experience Designer made its way to my eyes. From my background in Computer Science, I had previously heard of the career path, but never fully understood what one would do in it. Upon reading the job description, a new level of excitement developed for me. I found a lot of common ground between Business Intelligence and User Experience. From that moment on, I dedicated as much time as I could to learning, grow, and train in the field of UX. Following the LinkedIn Learning course, “Become a User Experience Designer“ started my first round of learning about the career path, and further solidified my desire in becoming a UXD, especially when it comes to Human and AI interactions, another field of interest from me.
