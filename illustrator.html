Illustrator for UX Design

Background

The final program taught in the LinkedIn’s Learning Path “Become a User Experience Designer “, was Illustrator. Following the “Illustrator for UX Design” I learned how to to set up your artboards and use grids, masks, and styles to fulfill common UX design needs, from organizing content and buttons to making a layout responsive.

Outcome

I am certainly not the strongest Illustrator user out there, but concepts such as making shapes, working with layers, and using guides were covered in an easy to digest way. If Illustrator was a requirement for a UX job of interest, I can say I have a decent grasp on it. I certainly would need more exposure to it than I have now, but I am confident that it is something that I can easily pick up again and master if needed.
